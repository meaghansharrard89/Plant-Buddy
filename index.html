Echinacea purpurea is an upright perennial that branches occasionally. Plants have fibrous roots and form small colonies from short thick rhizomes. Stems are sturdy and pubescent with leathery oval or lance shaped leaves. The blades are olive green with scattered teeth and a short winged petiole. Flower heads are borne on long leafless stalks. Each daisy-like head has 10-20 purplish-pink drooping rays. The ray florets surround a robust spiny orange cone made of many disc florets. In bright sunlight, flower heads are sometimes fragrant. Flowering begins in mid-summer and continues for about a month. Plants occasionally rebloom in autumn. Prickly clusters of dark achenes form from the disc florets and remain into early winter.
